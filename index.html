And now we are going to meet three unique letters. They are not used in the start of the word and they don't have own sound. But they play a very important role in Russian language. Let's meet them.
28. —ä

—ä is the rarest letter in the whole alphabet. It is used in the least amount of words. Though they are important. It doesn't actually have a sound but it makes letters (consonant letters only) sound absolutely solid. There are some rules how to put it correctly but it'll be a topic for another blog :]. In this blog I will mark it with UPPERCASE consonant which stands before it.

29. —ã

—ã is the only letter from these three which has a sound. It doesn't look like any of English sounds. More like a deeply stemmed 'i'. In this blog I will mark it as 'y'. It's also forbidden to put in the start of the word.

30. —å

Maybe —å is a very small letter without own sound it plays a huge role in russian language. Let it stay with any consonant (except –∂. —à. —Ü, but why, you will see later) and it will make it softer. In the next chapter there will be more detailed explanation of this thing.In this blog I will mark it with "'" after each consonant which is neighbour to —å.
